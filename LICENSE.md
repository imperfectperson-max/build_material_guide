## Post-Graduation Licensing Plans

Upon completion of the academic program and project evaluation (after June 2026), the project team will determine an appropriate open source license. Options under consideration include:

### Option 1: MIT License
**Characteristics:**
- ✅ Permissive license allowing commercial use
- ✅ Minimal restrictions on reuse
- ✅ Wide adoption in software community
- ✅ Simple and clear terms

**Suitable if:** Team wants maximum adoption and commercial opportunities

### Option 2: GNU General Public License v3.0 (GPL-3.0)
**Characteristics:**
- ✅ Copyleft license requiring derivatives to be open source
- ✅ Ensures contributions remain free and open
- ✅ Strong community protection
- ✅ Patent protection clauses

**Suitable if:** Team wants to ensure all derivatives remain open source

### Option 3: Apache License 2.0
**Characteristics:**
- ✅ Permissive license with patent protection
- ✅ Explicit grant of patent rights
- ✅ Requires preservation of copyright notices
- ✅ Industry-friendly terms

**Suitable if:** Team wants patent protection while remaining permissive

### Option 4: Creative Commons (Documentation Only)
**Characteristics:**
- ✅ Specifically for creative works and documentation
- ✅ Various options (CC BY, CC BY-SA, CC BY-NC)
- ✅ Easy to understand for non-technical users
- ✅ Global recognition

**Suitable for:** Documentation, research papers, datasets (separate from code)
